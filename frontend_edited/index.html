01 / Problem

Most scam transfers
look normal when
viewed alone.

Mule syndicates avoid simple rule engines by splitting money across many ordinary-looking accounts. MulePulse looks at the receiving network instead — who collects funds, how fast money leaves, which accounts share identifiers, and how close they are to known mule activity.

02 / How it works

From transaction noise
to a clear decision.

01

Ingest

Transaction records, account metadata, device signals, and known mule lists are pulled in and normalised into a time-aware event stream.

02

Connect

A graph engine links accounts, devices, and transfers, revealing coordinated receiving networks that look like isolated transactions at the account level.

03

Score

Clusters are ranked by velocity, fan-out ratio, known mule proximity, and network density. The highest-risk cluster reaches the analyst first.

04

Explain

A readable case file is generated for non-technical fraud teams — no model jargon, just plain-language reasoning: many sources, fast forwarding, known mule proximity.

05

Control

Every enforcement action — monitor, escalate, or freeze — requires a human decision. Every choice is logged, timestamped, and auditable for regulatory compliance.

03 / What sets it apart

Built for the
actual workflow
of a fraud analyst.

01

Plain-language alerts

Non-technical teams see exactly why a network is risky without reading model jargon. Every alert is written in language a bank leader can act on in a seven-minute demo.

02

Human approval loop

The AI recommends monitor, escalate, or freeze — but every action stays analyst-controlled. Full audit trail included for regulatory accountability.

03

Network evidence

Graph features reveal coordinated behavior that account-by-account rules miss entirely. Fan-out ratio, velocity, and proximity scored together.

04

Tunable thresholds

Analysts set their own alert comfort level. The system shows the real trade-off in plain language — more fraud caught vs. manageable daily caseload.
